Diving

Discover marine life and explore the deep blue.

Vancouver might be famous for its mountains and parks, but its underwater beauty is just as spectacular. Beneath the calm surface of Howe Sound and the Strait of Georgia lies a colourful marine world teeming with life, from plumose anemones and rockfish to playful seals and the legendary giant Pacific octopus. Scuba diving in Vancouver is more than an adventure; it’s an entry into a hidden realm of the Pacific Northwest.

One of the best-known spots for divers is Whytecliff Park, located in West Vancouver. It’s one of Canada’s first marine protected areas and offers easy shore access, making it ideal for beginners. More advanced divers often visit Porteau Cove, where sunken boats and artificial reefs attract a vibrant mix of sea creatures. Visibility is best during winter months, offering crystal-clear views of underwater rock walls and kelp forests.

If you’re new to diving, several local operators offer lessons and equipment rentals. Professional instructors ensure that even first-time divers feel safe and confident before exploring the depths. Most dive shops also organize guided tours, night dives, and photography sessions — so whether you’re diving solo or with friends, there’s always a new experience waiting.
